# Edinburgh Gardens 2026 physical-baseline and building audit

This note records the current 10 July 2026 baseline audit. The game clock remains the sole intentional 2030 element; park fabric, active works and vegetation are evaluated as 2026 conditions.

## Findings

- The archived Vicmap aerial is approximately 0.4 m/pixel at 1898 by 2048 pixels. It is strong evidence for persistent roof envelopes, paths, court footprints and enclosure/building relationships, but it predates the May 2026 tree removals and still shows six operational tennis courts. It must not be treated as a July 2026 vegetation or works-state photograph.
- The previously modelled 2030 completion layout was not a valid 2026 baseline. On 10 July 2026 the three northern tennis courts are an active works surface, the three southern clay courts remain operational, and the future two-court expansion, replacement planting and completed sports-pavilion facilities do not yet exist.
- The OSM sports-centre envelopes are not literal fence lines. At both tennis and bowls the clubhouse/outbuilding fabric closes part of the perimeter. Closing the polygon as fence geometry produced false fence and collision segments through buildings.
- CMP section 3.2.10 and Figures 76–77 show the old tennis pavilion as ochre-painted battened fibro-cement cladding above a weatherboard plinth, with red-brown trim, a gambrel-roofed north section, a skillion-roofed south addition, north/east verandahs, a bracketed south entrance awning and a round clock on the court-facing volume.
- CMP Figures 71–72 show the bowling club as a long glazed green-facing wing, a pale two-storey block, blue/gold fascia, extensive roof-mounted solar panels, a blue/maroon mural at the east end and brick Hannah memorial gates.
- CMP Figure 72 makes the Hannah entrance an architectural object in its own right: paired red-brick memorial piers on dark-brick bases, plaques and green metal gate fabric. Treating it as an empty gap in a generic chain-mesh run was materially incomplete.
- CMP Figure 36 shows the Kevin Murray Stand's cream/red ground facade, paired external stairs, long open upper gallery, white tiered seats, shallow light corrugated roof and central triangular pediment/roundel.
- CMP Figures 64–65 locate the Sportsman's Memorial's `IN MEMORIAM` panel, moulded pediment/swags and paired urn finials on its east elevation. The bronze wreath cabinet is on the west wall of the adjacent substation, not attached to a memorial column.
- Vicmap resolves the actual arbour approximately 25.8 real metres east/south-east of the Places of Pride pin, at `-37.788058, 144.980790`. The arbour is legible beside the square substation and immediately south of the exact bowling-club shell; the corrected `-0.106`-radian axis parallels that wall.
- CMP Figure 148 shows the Cook Memorial as a narrow grey-granite plinth with a shallow pyramidal cap, an arched bronze Cook portrait and two lower plaques. CMP section 3.10.6 confirms the separate *Endeavour* relief had already been removed before 2003 and must not be restored into the 2026 scene.
- CMP Figure 61 shows the Freeman Street object as an open timber entrance pavilion with two central passage bays, red-brown decorative eaves fretwork and a shallow corrugated hipped roof, not a solid gatehouse.
- CMP Figure 144 shows the Emely Baker Centre as a low single-storey tan-brick modernist building with aluminium/clerestory glazing, a metal tray-deck skillion roof and a tile-coped high-walled outdoor court. The current venue manual resolves the T-plan, long glass yard elevation, single and paired/sliding external doors, continuous outer wall, western vertical-bar gate and dark left-hand triangular sail.
- CMP Figure 145 identifies OSM way `242003562` as the 2010 Alfred Crescent Sports Pavilion, not a generic south amenities block. It documents the elongated butterfly/wraparound roof, curved southern public-toilet end, pale masonry, coloured cladding, clerestory, west glass entrance, north/east shutters, timber doors, changerooms, social room and kiosk. Council's later plan and completion report establish the added north-west public-toilet bank, exterior wash canopy, retained tank and two surviving south accessible toilets.
- CMP Figure 146 supports the earlier north toilet's corrugated enclosure and mixed sheet-metal/clear-plastic skillion. The later council plan and as-built photograph now resolve the completed external stall banks, accessible ramp, twin basins, grey signed doors, charcoal walls, perforated screens and exposed steel frame; the red proposal finish is not current. Public sources still do not resolve individual door, sign, gutter, vent, light or equipment positions on the seven bowling sheds.
- The CMP records exactly one surviving Fitzroy Council bollard at the Avenue B/St Georges Road entrance. The Rotunda-area historic lamps and later reproduction lamps had been removed by the CMP inspection. Policy pages prove rules, not the existence or coordinate of a physical rule sign.
- The 2004 CMP Figure 67 shows the picnic-area barbecue as a low, faceted bluestone-masonry unit with a timber-edged top and metal cooking surface, beside a drinking fountain. The current council page confirms that barbecues and drinking fountains remain Edinburgh Gardens facilities, but it does not prove that every 2026 unit is still the photographed 2004 installation.

## Implementation Translation

- Restored the park to the 2026 works state while retaining the `2030 EVENT` countdown clock as the only intentional anachronism.
- Georeferenced all 39 April/May 2026 removal-plan trees into world coordinates (0.766 game-unit RMS, approximately 0.6 m), suppressed those live-tree records and rendered their 2026 stumps.
- Rebuilt the major building silhouettes and facades from the CMP photographs without changing the painterly/anime material treatment:
  - multi-volume hipped/gabled tennis-pavilion roof, weatherboards, verandah, trim, separate porch door and round clock aligned over the main double doorway;
  - cream lower bowling wing with green-framed curtain glazing, pale upper storey, zincalume/solar roof, full blue-and-gold club fascia and east mural;
  - hipped Kevin Murray Stand roof, pediment/roundel, two oval-facing external stair flights and eight visible seating tiers;
  - shallow-hipped, fretwork-trimmed, open-passage Freeman Street entrance pavilion;
  - exact T-plan Emely Baker Blender asset with long tan-brick rear wing, projecting glazed community hall, tray-deck skillion, current external doors/sign, continuous tile-coped outer wall, western side gate and dark triangular shade sail;
  - exact current-plan Alfred Crescent Pavilion Blender asset with the black corrugated wraparound roof, curved green south-end fields, pale/dark masonry, long clerestories, deep timber-lined west canopy, paired entries, east shutters/kiosk, completed toilet extension, exterior basins/screen and retained tank;
  - exact-footprint north public-toilet Blender asset with current charcoal corrugation, 13 plan-derived external stall doors, accessible ramp, twin exterior basins, perforated upper screens, exposed steel and alternating opaque/translucent skillion strips;
  - aerial-fitted Sportsman's Memorial Blender asset with six detailed Tuscan columns/pedestals, moulded entablature/rafters, rectangular swag panel/urns, south dedication, restored wreath/name panel and team-photo context.
- Corrected the Sportsman's Memorial from the roof-intersecting AWM pin to the Vicmap-visible structure at `-37.788058, 144.980790`, aligned it parallel to the club, and replaced the old solid/triangular proxy with the 24,930-triangle editable/runtime artifact. Six pedestal blockers and a tightly scoped centre–east bay preserve entry despite the game's unusually broad movement proxy; the club and west substation remain closed.
- Rebuilt the Cook Memorial face with its arched bronze portrait relief, two surviving lower plaques and pyramidal granite cap while leaving the long-removed *Endeavour* relief absent.
- Removed unsupported bowling-shed vents, loose equipment, barrels, crates and duplicate roofs. The sheds now retain their exact OSM/aerial envelopes without asserting unverified sub-details.
- Superseded the earlier generic south-amenities treatment after identifying it as Alfred Crescent Sports Pavilion. OSM version 5 remains the exact 2026 shell; the CMP, council asset photograph, architect portfolio and completed toilet works now control the previously unresolved facade and extension details.
- Removed invented exterior appliances and freestanding room props from building interactions. Visible access panels now snap to the linked wall and align with its facade; interaction triggers remain outside collision so they are usable.
- Split the tennis and bowling fence runs at the documented building closures, retained the Court 3 south-east and Hannah gate gaps, and sampled both gate-to-clubroom routes against every building/fence blocker.
- Rebuilt the Hannah memorial entrance at its mapped gate point with paired brick piers, dark bases, capstones, plaques and visibly open green gate leaves. The piers have matching collision while the central pedestrian route remains clear.
- Made the entrance pavilion's two central bays passable, kept mapped buildings solid, aligned the grandstand and Rotunda climb interactions with their rendered stair flights, retained both structure-specific collision bypasses, and retained the CMP-documented non-original steel gate at the Rotunda stair entrance.
- Removed unsupported future structures, extra courts, replacement trees, policy signs, generic entrance furniture, picnic tables, duplicate sports-prism blockers, historic/reproduction lamps, extra cast-iron bollards and interpretive signs without public coordinates.
- Rebuilt both mapped barbecue units from CMP Figure 67 as low faceted bluestone bodies with visible mortar courses, a timber rim, stainless cooking top, dark hotplate and controls, while retaining their OSM coordinates and the existing painterly/anime material language.
- Generated `docs/research/edinburgh-gardens-2026-object-audit-ledger.json`: 579 fixed physical entries, 43 gameplay entries, 107 non-object render treatments and 14 movable scenic entries. Fifty-three physical entries remain explicitly unresolved rather than silently presented as survey-accurate. The Alfred pass replaces one generic access point with researched clubroom/kiosk access, the north-toilet pass adds two evidence-linked external stall banks, and the memorial pass adds the physical east-inscription access point. Semantic interaction halos remain outside the fixed-object count.
- Added `scripts/generate-aerial-audit-overlay.mjs` to project every current path, building, fence, tree, amenity and detail over the official aerial, plus a per-bench contact sheet.
- Added a deterministic headless Three.js audit that builds every mapped-building preview plus the Kevin Murray Stand, Rotunda, north toilets and memorials. It checks for empty/non-finite/exploded geometry, implausible height, lost architectural detail, and facade materials mirrored behind their source-backed frontage. This exercises the same `WorldBuilder` geometry used by the browser without substituting a second model implementation.
- Added an exhaustive browser-rendered physical-object visual audit. Every one of the 579 ledger objects is required to resolve to exactly one `WorldBuilder` preview and render from at least two angles. Major buildings/landmarks support four-angle review; the new memorial landmark received four refreshed views and its east-inscription object two linked-context views, both with zero signal issues. Long paths and hardscape lines combine an aerial whole-object view with a low detail view.
- The visual review found and corrected painterly vertex-colour omissions that made several primitive amenities render black, coarse terrain-conforming polygons that partially buried Court 3 and other large surfaces, inadequate football-goal and tree framing, and context-free building interaction previews. These were render/audit defects rather than source-placement changes; the painterly/anime style itself was retained.

## Uncertainty

- The Vicmap aerial acquisition date is not exposed in the WMS response used here. Its visible six-court/tree state proves it predates the July 2026 baseline. It is used only where later 2026 evidence does not contradict it.
- CMP text records modern floodlights on the Rotunda entablature, but neither CMP Figure 142 nor the current official Yarra venue photographs resolve their count and angular positions. No evenly spaced proxy lights are added; their exact placement remains a building-detail data gap.
- Public sources do not provide a current surveyed point or measured design for every garden bed, awning/eave, oval gate width, bench orientation or building sub-detail. The 53 `unresolved-public-data-gap` entries retain 26 OSM bench headings plus explicit building, landmark, fence and parking-detail gaps; Alfred Crescent Pavilion is no longer in that unresolved-building group.
- The tennis pavilion/clubhouse was inaccessible and in a May 2026–March 2027 relocation/modernisation package. Public updates establish staging but not a daily site survey for 10 July. The CMP can support a reusable heritage-building asset, but not its exact baseline coordinate or partial-demolition state; an integrated replacement asset is therefore deferred pending dated construction imagery.
- The seven bowling outbuildings retain reliable footprints but insufficient public facade photography for individual doors, signs and equipment. Plain envelopes remain more accurate than fabricated detail there.
- Figure 67 is historical design evidence, not a dated 2026 condition survey. The current council facility list confirms barbecue provision, and OSM fixes the two mapped coordinates, but any undocumented replacement of the photographed masonry units remains a public-data gap.
- The supported embedded browser connection was unavailable, so visual QA used the repository's Playwright/Chromium renderer against the same runtime `WorldBuilder`. This produced actual WebGL frames rather than a substitute model, but it did not replace interactive first-person traversal; navigation remains covered by collision/access simulations and the structure interaction tests.
