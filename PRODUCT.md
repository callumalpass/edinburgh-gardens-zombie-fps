# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Players running a browser or desktop first-person survival game, including small LAN co-op groups. They need to understand their survivor identity, enter a run quickly, read combat state at a glance, and distinguish teammates and zombie archetypes under changing Melbourne winter light and weather.

## Product Purpose

Edinburgh Gardens 2030 turns a recognisable, research-grounded Fitzroy North park into a stylised survival playground. Success means the real place remains legible, the combat and co-op systems remain responsive, and authored characters strengthen rather than overwhelm the painterly low-poly world.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, local, and scrappy. The game should feel like a handmade illustrated account of a familiar Melbourne place under pressure, with warmth and wit surviving inside the danger.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic military-shooter interfaces, glossy hero-selection grids, photorealistic gore, neon cyberpunk styling, and anonymous asset-store characters. Avoid decorative UI that slows entry into a run or makes accessibility states difficult to read.

## Design Principles

1. Make Fitzroy specificity visible in silhouettes, materials, clothing, names, and copy.
2. Let selection and status interfaces serve play first, with identity expressed through restrained, purposeful detail.
3. Keep visual models, collision, hit zones, and network state explicitly separated so art iteration cannot destabilise gameplay.
4. Prefer a small cast of memorable, reusable forms over a large catalogue of minor variants.
5. Validate authored assets both as isolated designs and in the live park under representative combat load.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Preserve keyboard operation, visible focus, semantic labels, the existing high-contrast HUD option, and reduced-motion behaviour. Character identity must not depend on colour alone, and selection layouts must remain usable at narrow browser widths.
